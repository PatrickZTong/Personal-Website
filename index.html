Accepted Conference Papers

Accepted · IAMCR 2026 (POL)

Exclusionary Populism in the U.S.: From Party-wide Patterns to Trump’s Rhetoric

Galway, Ireland · June–July 2026

Developed a party-month panel (1952–2024) using the Annenberg Presidential Database and LIWC-based text measures to track exclusionary populist rhetoric in U.S. campaign speeches.

Modeled partisan differences in “they–we” framing and dynamic effects of elite- and people-centered cues, and constructed an Exclusionary Populism Index (EPI) to characterize rhetorical shifts in the Trump era.

Methods: Longitudinal panel analysis, LIWC lexicon-based indicators, reproducible Python workflow, visualization.

Accepted · IAMCR 2026 (ETH)

When the Dead Seem Present: Perceived Realism, Social Presence, and Psychological Mechanisms in AI-based Digital Resurrection

Galway, Ireland · June–July 2026

Proposed an ethics-centered framework for AI-based digital resurrection, testing how perceived realism affects behavioral intention through emotional authenticity/comfort and uncanny discomfort under varying levels of social presence.

Designed a preregistered between-subjects online experiment in Qualtrics with a three-condition fidelity manipulation and an intended sample of approximately 600 adults in mainland China.

Methods: Survey experiment, moderated parallel mediation, robustness checks for attrition, covariate imbalance, and scale validity.

Submitted Papers

Submitted · NCA 2026 (Political Communication)

Speaking as the People, the Protector, or the Provocateur: Rhetorical Persona Types in U.S. Presidential Campaign Speeches (1952–2024)

Examines whether presidential candidates speak through recurring rhetorical personae rather than a single populism continuum, using multidimensional indicators of people-centered, anti-elite, policy-oriented, and threat-based rhetoric.

Uses latent profile analysis (LPA) to identify speech-level persona types, derive candidate-level signatures, and evaluate cross-party and historical variation.

Methods: LIWC-based text analysis, latent profile analysis, candidate-period comparative modeling.

Submitted · NCA 2026 (Family Communication)

When Home Moves Away: The Causal Effect of Parental Rural-to-Urban Migration on Rural-Origin Children’s Intergenerational Ties in China

Investigates how parental migration arrangements (left-behind, co-migrant, non-migrant) shape intergenerational ties among rural-origin children in China.

Uses CFPS panel data to estimate impacts on communication, emotional attention, parental monitoring, and conflict.

Methods: Propensity-score matching (PSM), difference-in-differences (DiD), panel causal inference.
